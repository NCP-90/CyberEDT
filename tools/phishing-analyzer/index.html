Why is Phishing Dangerous?

  • Credential Theft: Attackers steal usernames and passwords to gain unauthorized access to accounts.
  • Malware Installation: Clicking a link can trigger automatic downloading of malicious software.
  • Financial Fraud: Fake banking sites trick users into transferring money or revealing credit card details.

Common Attack Patterns

Typosquatting

Using g0ogle.com instead of google.com to mislead users.

Subdomain Abuse

Hiding the real domain deep in subdomains, e.g., paypal.secure-update.com.

Homograph Attack

Using foreign characters that look identical, e.g., Cyrillic а vs Latin a.

URL Shorteners

Masking malicious links behind services like bit.ly or tinyurl.com.

Brand Impersonation

Mimicking trusted brands in the URL path, e.g., login-apple-id.com.

Extension Spoofing

Disguising malware as documents, e.g., invoice.pdf.exe.
